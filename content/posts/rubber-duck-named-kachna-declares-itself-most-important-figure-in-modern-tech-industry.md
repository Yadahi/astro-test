## The Duck Who Debugs

In a modest corner of a bustling tech office in San Francisco, a rubber duck named Kachna sits atop a mechanical keyboard, silently judging every line of code written within a three-monitor radius. Her expression — frozen somewhere between wisdom and mild indigestion — has reportedly inspired more software breakthroughs than any Ivy League computer science degree in recent memory.

"I just talk to her," explained senior developer Marcus Holt, 34, who adopted Kachna from a Prague gift shop in 2021. "I explain my code out loud, and halfway through my second sentence, I realize what I've done wrong. She never says a word. She doesn't have to. Her silence is *devastating*." The technique, known in developer circles as "rubber duck debugging," involves explaining a problem aloud to an inanimate object in order to trigger one's own epiphany. Kachna, however, has taken the practice to legendary levels.

## A Rising Star (and a Rising Ego)

Word of Kachna's talents spread quickly through the office, and she has since been unofficially added to the company org chart somewhere between "VP of Engineering" and "Office Plant (deceased)." Colleagues report that Kachna has attended 47 sprint planning meetings, two all-hands presentations, and one very uncomfortable performance review. HR has declined to comment.

Kachna's influence has not gone without controversy. Junior developer Priya Nair claims the duck "looked at her funny" when she pushed untested code to production last Tuesday. "It wasn't accusatory, exactly," said Nair, visibly shaken. "It was more like... disappointment. Pure, yellow, squeaky disappointment." The offending code was rolled back within the hour.

## What's Next for the World's Most Influential Duck

As of press time, Kachna was unavailable for comment, having been knocked behind the monitor during a particularly aggressive typing session. She is expected to re-emerge during the next debugging crisis, which engineering leads estimate will be sometime this afternoon. A GoFundMe has been launched to purchase Kachna a second rubber duck so she "has someone to talk her own problems through with" — a campaign that has, perhaps not surprisingly, already exceeded its funding goal by 400 percent.
